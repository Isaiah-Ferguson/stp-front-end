# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Admins** (primary): one or two staff who own the full system — enrollment, document compliance, reporting, and cross-program oversight. They open this daily to manage tasks, track expiring certifications, and get a quick read on program health.

**Teachers and coordinators** (secondary): program leads for MJC, Pathways, Manteca PT, and Productions who need scoped views for their cohort — logging attendance, reviewing participant status, and tracking their own tasks.

Both roles use the tool in a professional non-profit context: shared computers, possibly tablets in the classroom, goal-oriented sessions rather than casual browsing.

## Product Purpose

An internal CRM for a performing-arts non-profit that serves youth participants across four programs. It centralizes participant records, staff compliance (POS, CPR, TB clearances), attendance tracking, task management, calendar, and reporting in one workspace.

Success means: staff spend less time chasing documents and more time with participants. Every alert surfaces the right action. Every page answers "what do I need to do today?"

## Brand Personality

Friendly, colorful, approachable. The non-profit mission is warm and human — the tool should reflect that. It has energy and care without being loud or playful. Think a well-run community program office: organized, personal, not corporate.

Three words: **warm, purposeful, alive**.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / corporate**: Salesforce, Jira, gray-on-white enterprise dashboards. Cold palette, no personality, feels like a vendor product.
- **Overly playful / consumer**: rounded-everything, emoji-heavy, pastel overload — wrong for a professional daily tool.
- **Dark / terminal aesthetic**: wrong mood entirely for this context and audience.

## Design Principles

1. **The mission shows in the design.** This serves a non-profit working with youth. The warmth and care of that work should be legible in the interface — not through decoration, but through tone, color, and human language.
2. **Surface the right action.** Every page should answer "what needs my attention right now?" Alerts, tasks, and status are first-class citizens, not afterthoughts.
3. **Accessible by default.** WCAG 2.1 AA throughout. Color is never the only signal — always paired with icon or label. Tap targets ≥34px for tablet use.
4. **Calm density.** Staff are in the tool all day. The interface should feel organized and scannable, not cluttered. Space earns its place.
5. **Program identity matters.** The four program colors (MJC blue, Pathways teal, Manteca coral, Productions amber) are meaningful to staff — they map to real teams and real relationships. Use them consistently and respectfully.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum: 4.5:1 body text contrast, 3:1 large text/UI components.
- Color is never the only differentiator — always paired with icon, label, or shape.
- Keyboard navigable throughout.
- Screen-reader labels on all interactive elements.
- Participants are always referred to as **participants**, never "clients" or "cases". Tone is warm, plain, and respectful.
